Offering A Hand To Those In Need

People who are experiencing homelessness are often unfairly stereotyped because of social media. We often see them depicted in a negative light and categorized as lazy, mentally ill, and aggressive. As a result, it makes it more difficult for those in need to gain the help that they need. This is where you can lend a helping hand to help those in need.

What Is Homelessness?

Homelessness is a condition where individuals or families lack a stable and secure place to live. As of 2023, there are over half a million people experiencing homelessness in the United States alone and many more around the world. There are many causes of homelessness including, the lack of affordable housing, unemployment, and low wages. Although there are temporary shelters available, most homeless individuals still reside in places that are not meant for habitation. Even with access to available shelters, there are often safety concerns that turn many homeless people away from seeking help from these shelters. As a result, homelessness not only deprives people of a stable and safe place to live but also exposes them to numerous health risks, social exclusion, and a cycle of poverty that can be difficult to escape. Homelessness is not only an issue for adults but also for many children and young adults.

Statistics On Homelessness



653,104 people experienced homelessness during the 2023 Point-in-Time (PIT) Count. This figure represents a record high since homelessness data collection began in 2007.
Source: National Alliance To End Homelessness.




60% of Continuums of Care reported an increase in homelessness among unaccompanied youth between 2022 and 2023. The New York City CoC indicated the largest increase, having 75% more unaccompanied youth experiencing homelessness than in 2022.
Source: National Alliance To End Homelessness.




Homelessnes increased by 12% between the 2022 and 2023 PIT Counts. Another record in the history of data collection, the previous high was only 3 percent and occurred between 2018 and 2019.
Source: National Alliance To End Homelessness

Ways You Can Help Those In Need

  • Donate: Offer clothing, blankets, and non-perishable food to shelters or directly to individuals.
  • Volunteer: Help at shelters or soup kitchens, offering time and skills to assist the homeless community.
  • Advocate: Support policies that address homelessness and raise awareness about the issue.
  • Provide Support: Offer a listening ear or companionship to those experiencing homelessness.
  • Fundraise: Organize or contribute to fundraisers for homeless shelters or organizations aiding the homeless.
